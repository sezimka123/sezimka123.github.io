Floristics for beginners.

Floristics, or floral design is the art of making bouquets and other compositions from various natural materials-flowers, leaves, herbs, fruits, etc. Some manage to create bouquets from dried fruits, soft toys, sweets, and even socks. To become a professional florist, you need to pass special training. But after studying this article, you will be able to make an amazingly beautiful bouquet yourself.

Principles of making floral compositions.

In floristry, as in any other art, there are rules and principles that must be strictly followed to create a beautiful composition that pleases the eye. Even very experienced florists don't make up bouquets at random.Ideally, a floral arrangement should meet the following requirements: harmony, that is, all elements form a single whole; the design corresponds to the chosen style; a variety of textures and structures of plants; a well-chosen color scheme, and here you will help the color circle; dynamism, the presence of one or more focal points; compliance with the Golden ratio rule; balance of all parts of the composition. We will analyze each quality in more detail, so that you can freely navigate the theory of Floristics.

Styles.

Vegetative style assumes that the composition should be as close as possible to the natural image. This style is characterized by a muted color palette, often using twigs, berries, moss and other natural materials. It is important to make sure that the plants belong to the same season. This is a very difficult style for beginners.

Decorative style. Any combination of plants is allowed here, as long as they look organic, and none of them loses its zest. In decorative compositions, you can safely use the most unusual materials for finishing, the more textured they are, the better. Often used for decoration of premises and preparation of compositions with a floral sponge.

Linear style. The composition is built on the principle: clear lines, minimum materials, maximum naturalness. Most often, such compositions use plants with curved or long stems, and the elements are arranged asymmetrically.

Massive style. The composition has a clear shape. All elements are tightly connected to each other or to the frame. There are no strictly defined focus points, colors, size of buds and lines smoothly merge into each other.
